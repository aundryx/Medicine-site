Sambong (Blumea balsamifera)

Blumea balsafimera, also known as sambong, is a native herb in the Philippines, is well-known for its diuretic properties, for its diuretic which play a significant role in managing fever. The plant contains active compounds such as flavonoids, volatile oils, and alkaloids, which are believed to help reduce fever by promoting the excretion of excess fluids and toxins from the body. By facilitating the removal of toxins, sambong supports the body's natural detoxification process, reducing inflammation that can contribute to fever

Numerous studies and traditional practices have recognized sambong's role in treating fevers. According to the Philippine Journal of Science, sambong's active compounds have been shown to exert anti-inflammatory and antipyretic (fever-reducing) effects

Guava leaves

Guava leaves have long been used in the Philippines as a natural remedy for fever, primarily due to their antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties. The leaves contain compounds like flavonoids, tannins, and essential oils, which contribute to their medicinal effects. Brewed into a tea, guava leaves help reduce fever by promoting sweat, thereby assisting the body in cooling down and eliminating toxins associated with infections. Additionally, the leaves have been traditionally used to support recovery from colds, respiratory infections, and gastrointestinal issues, which often accompany fever

Lemon balm

Lemon balm or Melissa officinalis has been used traditionally for its antiviral and antimicrobial properties, making it a popular herb for treating mild fevers, especially those caused by viral infections like colds and flu. Its active compounds, including polyphenols and essential oils, help promote sweating (diaphoretic action), which can aid in reducing body temperature. A study published in Phytomedicine highlighted that lemon balm can help reduce fever by enhancing the body's ability to regulate temperature through increased sweating. Additionally, its antiviral properties contribute to its effectiveness in managing viral infections that commonly cause fevers
