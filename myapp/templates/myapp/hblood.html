Bitter melon

Ampalaya, commonly referred to as bitter melon, is a well-known herbal remedy in the Philippines used for managing high blood pressure (hypertension). This plant has been utilized in traditional medicine for centuries due to its potential health benefits, particularly in addressing metabolic conditions like hypertension. The efficacy of Ampalaya in lowering blood pressure is largely attributed to its bioactive compounds, such as charantin, polypeptides, and vicine, which contribute to improved insulin sensitivity and reduced glucose levels

Garlic

Garlic, also known as bawang, has long been a part of traditional medicine in the Philippines, particularly for managing high blood pressure. The active compound allicin found in garlic is widely recognized for its beneficial effects on cardiovascular health. Allicin helps relax blood vessels, reducing the resistance to blood flow, which contributes to lower blood pressure. Research has shown that garlic supplementation can lead to significant reductions in both systolic and diastolic blood pressure, comparable to some pharmaceutical treatments. Furthermore, garlic's antioxidant properties help combat oxidative stress and inflammation, both of which are major contributors to hypertension

Banaba leaves

Banaba leaves, derived from the Lagerstroemia speciosa plant, have long been used in traditional medicine, particularly in the Philippines, as a remedy for various health issues, including high blood pressure and diabetes. The leaves contain corosolic acid, a compound known for its beneficial effects on insulin sensitivity and glucose metabolism. Corosolic acid helps improve blood sugar control, which in turn supports better cardiovascular health and may contribute to lowering high blood pressure
