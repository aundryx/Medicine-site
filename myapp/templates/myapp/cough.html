Ginger

Ginger is another well-known herbal remedy commonly used to manage cough symptoms. Its anti-inflammatory and mucolytic properties help reduce throat irritation and mucus production, making it effective for relieving persistent coughing. According to a meta-analysis published in The Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews, ginger can be beneficial in reducing coughing and improving overall respiratory health

Licorice Root

Licorice root contains compounds that help thin mucus and soothe the respiratory tract. It has been traditionally used to relieve coughing and inflammation in the throat. Research published in Journal of Ethnopharmacology suggests licorice root can help reduce coughing and helps mucus clearance

Marshmallow Root

Marshmallow root has long been used for its soothing properties, particularly for dry, irritating coughs. It contains mucilage, a gel-like substance that coats the throat, reducing irritation and promoting a smoother respiratory tract. Studies, including those published in European Journal of Integrative Medicine, show that marshmallow root effectively reduces coughing and throat discomfort

Thyme

Thyme is widely recognized for its soothing properties, especially in treating coughs caused by mucus buildup and throat irritation. The active compounds in thyme, particularly thymol, exhibit antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects, reducing throat discomfort and easing coughing. A systematic review published in Phytotherapy Research found that thyme-based formulations, such as syrups, are effective in reducing both the frequency and severity of coughs, especially in cases of upper respiratory infections
